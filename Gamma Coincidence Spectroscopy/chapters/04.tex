\section{Energy Resolution}

The energy resolution $\Delta E / E$ describes the ability of a detector to distinguish between two nearby energy lines. It is defined using the full width at half maximum (FWHM) of a photopeak,

\begin{equation}
\Delta E = 2\sqrt{2\ln 2},\sigma \approx 2.355,\sigma ,
\end{equation}

where $\sigma$ is obtained from the Gaussian fits.

Since the number of generated charge carriers is proportional to the deposited energy and follows Poisson statistics, one expects

\begin{equation}
\frac{\Delta E}{E} \propto \frac{1}{\sqrt{E}} .
\end{equation}

To test this behavior, the relative energy resolution was calculated for all measured photopeaks of both detectors. The resulting values are listed in table \ref{tab:energy_resolution}, and $\Delta E/E$ is plotted as a function of $1/\sqrt{E}$ in figure \ref{fig:energy_resolution}.

    \caption{Relative energy resolution $\Delta E / E$ as a function of $1/\sqrt{E}$ for the Ge semiconductor detector (left) and the NaI scintillator (right). A linear fit through the origin of the form $\Delta E/E = \alpha/\sqrt{E}$ is shown in each panel.}

A linear fit through the origin of the form
\begin{equation}
\frac{\Delta E}{E} = \frac{\alpha}{\sqrt{E}}
\end{equation}

was applied. The fit yields

\begin{align}
\alpha_\text{Ge} &= 0.37 \pm 0.05 \sqrt{\text{keV}}, \\
\alpha_\text{NaI} &= 3.82 \pm 0.20 \sqrt{\text{keV}}.
\end{align}

The Ge semiconductor detector shows a significantly smaller resolution coefficient than the NaI scintillator, corresponding to much narrower photopeaks. This agrees with the spectra discussed earlier, where the Ge detector clearly resolves close-lying peaks, while the NaI detector exhibits broader structures.

The measured data follow the expected $1/\sqrt{E}$ dependence reasonably well for both detectors. This indicates that statistical fluctuations in the number of generated charge carriers are the dominant factor limiting the energy resolution in the investigated energy range.
